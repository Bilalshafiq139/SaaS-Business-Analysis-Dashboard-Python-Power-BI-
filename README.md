# SaaS Business Analysis Dashboard

This project analyzes SaaS acquisition listings to identify valuation patterns, profitability signals, growth segments, and potential acquisition opportunities.

The project combines Python-based data cleaning and exploratory analysis with a Power BI dashboard. It is designed as a business analytics case study for SaaS investors, operators, and analysts.

## Business Questions

- Which SaaS listings show the strongest profitability relative to asking price?
- Do higher-growth companies receive higher revenue multiples?
- How do profit margins differ across growth segments?
- Which locations appear attractive after accounting for sample size?
- What risks or caveats should an investor consider before using these rankings?

## Key Findings

- Annual revenue has a stronger relationship with asking price than annual profit, with a correlation of 0.75 compared with 0.51.
- Growth rate has almost no relationship with revenue multiple in this sample, with a correlation of -0.06.
- High-growth listings show a median profit margin of 34%, compared with 10% for low-growth listings.
- The dataset contains 7 listings with negative annual profit, so payback analysis should only be applied to profitable listings.
- Country-level rankings are directional because several locations have very small sample sizes.

## Metrics Used

| Metric | Definition | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Profit margin | `totalProfitAnnual / totalRevenueAnnual` | Measures operating profitability |
| Profit-to-price ratio | `totalProfitAnnual / askingPrice` | Estimates annual profit yield relative to acquisition price |
| Payback years | `askingPrice / totalProfitAnnual` | Estimates years needed to recover purchase price from current profit |
| Revenue multiple | `askingPrice / totalRevenueAnnual` or listed multiple | Compares asking price against revenue scale |
| Growth category | Low, medium, or high annual growth bucket | Segments companies by growth profile |

## Data Cleaning Summary

- Converted listing and founding dates into datetime fields.
- Filled missing growth values using the median growth rate.
- Filled missing locations with `Unknown`.
- Removed rows missing listing headlines.
- Created reproducible business metrics for profitability, payback, growth segment, and valuation.

## Limitations

- The dataset is a small sample of SaaS listings and should not be treated as a complete market view.
- Some locations have only one listing, which makes country-level comparisons unreliable.
- Extreme growth values can distort averages, especially for early-stage businesses.
- Asking prices are seller-provided and may not represent final transaction prices.
- Qualitative factors such as churn, retention, customer concentration, and product defensibility are not available in the dataset.
